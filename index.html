Scoring Rules
⛔
Triple Cap: Max number of strokes a player can take on a given hole (Par + 3 + strokes received)
🔢
Double Cap: Used to calculate adjusted gross score for handicap purposes (Par + 2 + strokes received)
🎯
Playing HCP: 90% of course handicap for all formats
📋
DSN vs GHIN: Same core formula, three key differences: (1) No 0.96 multiplier — GHIN multiplies final HI by 0.96; DSN doesn't, so DSN HIs run ~4% higher (about +0.8 strokes at HI 20). (2) No exceptional score reduction — GHIN can auto-lower your HI after a great round; DSN doesn't. (3) No soft/hard cap — GHIN limits how fast your HI can rise; DSN has no ceiling. Net result: DSN HIs are slightly more generous than GHIN by design — keeps it simple and consistent for everyone regardless of GHIN membership.
📊
Stableford: Net bogey=1, par=2, birdie=4, eagle+=8. Highest wins.
🏌️
Best Ball: Team best net per hole. Pairs seeded by standings (1st+8th, etc).
⚔️
4-Ball Match Play: Stroke diff vs low handicap in group. Most holes won wins.
🔄
H2 Reset: H2 points start fresh at zero. Season total is cumulative.
📐 How Your Handicap Index is Calculated
1️⃣
Adjusted Gross: Each hole is capped at Par + 2 + strokes received before anything is calculated. This prevents one blowup hole from wrecking your handicap.
2️⃣
9-Hole Differential: Measures how good your round was relative to the course difficulty: (Adj Gross − Course Rating) × 113 ÷ Slope. Lower = better round.
Front: Rating 33.5, Slope 117  ·  Back: Rating 33.7, Slope 118
3️⃣
18-Hole Equivalent: Since we only play 9 holes, we estimate what you would have shot on the other 9 by adding HI ÷ 2 (your expected average 9). This converts your round into a full 18-hole number so it can be compared across all rounds.
4️⃣
HI Calculation: Take the best 8 of your last 20 18-hole equivalents and average them. If you have fewer than 8 rounds, all rounds are averaged. Best 8 of 20 means a bad round doesn't drag you down for long.
5️⃣
Course HCP: Converts your HI to this specific course: round(HI × (Slope ÷ 113) ÷ 2 + (Rating − Par)). The ÷ 2 is critical — we're playing 9 holes, not 18.
6️⃣
Playing HCP: Course HCP × 90% — the number of strokes you actually receive. The 10% reduction keeps competition tighter.
🔒
HI Snapshot: Your HI is locked the moment you enter your first score each week. Stroke allocation for that week won't change even if your HI updates later. Shown with a 🔒 badge on score cards.
